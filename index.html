About me

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at Virginia Tech, advised by Dr. Peng Gao.
I got the M.S. degree in Chemistry from King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST) in 2022. I received the B.S. degree in Chemical Science from Southern University of Science and Technology (SUSTech).

Research

My research interest lies in machine learning and AI for science.
I develop AI/ML models informed by fundamental scientific principles to accelerate the design and discovery of novel materials and small molecules. My work focuses on integrating physical laws such as symmetry, and theories, into AI architectures to enhance model interpretability, efficiency, and predictive accuracy. By bridging AI and the science, I aim to advance foundational AI methodologies that drive innovation in materials and molecules design and beyond.
